Supporting the Raspberry Pi Foundation.

The non-profit Raspberry Pi Foundation creates ultra low-cost single-board computers (SBCs) for educational use. Its SBCs are the most popular in the world, which is why we've developed the VeinCam kit around them. You'll need a Pi 3 Model A+ board (~US$25) for this project.

You'll also need the 'Pi NoIR (V2)' infrared-sensitive HD camera (~US$24) for this project.

The links above will take you to a page with a 'buy' button that connects you to local distributors.

Compatible microSD cards.

Your Raspberry Pi requires software loaded onto a microSD card (like below) to run. They sell for under US$10 on eBay or Amazon. An 8GB card or larger will do the trick. One with faster read speeds will be better.

If you want, we can supply a compatible microSD card with the required software pre-loaded on it. Just select that option when you choose your kit.

Compatible power sources.

Once assembled, your VeinCam will need a power source of 5V at 2.5A (into the microUSB port of the Raspberry Pi). It doesn't care how it gets that, so you could use a wall adapter or a portable power bank and cable.

You probably already have one for charging a mobile phone. Just check that it outputs 5V at ~2.5A, with a cable going to microUSB, and you'll be good to go. Your local electronics store will also stock what you need.
